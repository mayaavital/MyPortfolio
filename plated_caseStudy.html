Plated: A recipe discovery & cooking app

Background & Project Overview

Plated was a group project for my course on human computer interaction design. We were tasked to pick a domain and follow the 5 stage design process. In my role I oversaw and participated in every step of the design and development, from organizing team materials to helping implement databases and implementing the mobile interface, I made significant contributions to our final product and process.

Problem Statement

During our needfinding process we explored the domain of cooking and found that many amateur chefs find cooking to be a tedious chore that often takes longer than expected and lacks creativity.

Plated addresses this by offering a curated selection of user-shared recipes tailored to individual preferences and available ingredients. Additionally, the app features an in-app virtual chef assistant, Chef Su, who provides guidance and answers common cooking questions. By fostering creativity and collaboration, Plated transforms cooking into a more enjoyable and accessible experience for chefs of all skill levels.

Through needfinding interviews with diverse participants, we explored the challenges and motivations of amateur cooks to uncover key insights that inform the design of effective cooking solutions. Out of 4 hour long interviews in the span of a week, distinct patterns and needs emerged. Chef N emphasized the importance of accessible, flexible recipes that spark curiosity and allow cooking to become a form of self-expression. Travis highlighted the need for meal preparation to align with specific nutritional goals while being time-efficient and less of a chore. The Philz Customer shared the challenge of cooking as an unenjoyable task and stressed the importance of creating quick, easy meals that satisfy her family. These insights underscored that time, knowledge gaps, and motivation are significant barriers for amateur cooks, and solutions that simplify cooking while fostering creativity and enjoyment could be transformative.

Design Process

After creating our interview protocol, we recruited interview participants. Most of them were randomly approached, only one was recruited for their expertise in the cooking and cooking education domain. After conducting several interviews, we frameworked the data in an empathy map in order to make sense of it.

With the data from our interview analysis, we created POV statements for our most engaging interviews (Jad, Philz customer, and Travis), while also keeping in mind diversity of thought and background. After we created each POV, we rapidly brainstormed 35 HMW statements for Jad, Philz customer, and Travis each. We boiled down the 35 HMW per interviewee to the 2-3 most interesting, and from there brainstormed solutions. We came away with three viable solutions: Dinner Party Planning App, Food tinder, and Su chef.


Our final solution, Plated, focuses on enhancing the cooking experience for amateur chefs by offering a curated selection of recipes based on their pantry items and preferences. The app empowers users to discover new recipes, manage their ingredient inventory, and even contribute their own creations, fostering creativity and independence in the kitchen. This solution reflects our mission to make cooking both enjoyable and accessible, addressing user frustrations with uninspiring meal preparation.

First we built a low fidelity prototype by sketching about 21 screens that covered the functionality of our app across different task flows. We conducted usability testing on four potential users from different demographics and with different backgrounds. With our results we went onto building our medium fidelity prototype in Figma, receiving design feedback from HCI Phd students halfway through the development. Before implementing our prototype in react native, our figma prototype underwent heuristic evaluation process. After fixing our heuristic violations, we began developing our high-fi prototype through React Native and included almost every feature we initially set out to develop. During the development process I performed overall polishing of the entire app along with implementing multiple features myself. First I did the chef su chat bot integration, I used a Gemini API to accomplish this and kept our interface simple but on brand. To facilitate easy use, I included potential chat prompts and an informational page with more details about how to use our chat bot, along with disclosures about the Gemini API.

Additionally I implemented the user profile page, this page is designed to be the hub for recipe access with options to organize all recipes into "cookbooks", along with sections displaying your personal, saved, and recreation recipes. I used Supabase Java based Client library commands to render recipes from our database belonging in each category in their recipe boxes, along with the corresponding recipe data on each recipe details page. Outcome

All this work culminated in our final project expo where we were able to speak about our product to the project judges, other students, interested faculty, and industry folks. This served as an opportunity to practice speaking about our product in a way that translates our vision to potential users, while also allowing us to get authentic feedback for us to use with any future work on this project. During this expo we ran demos of the application and allowed users to post "recipes" in real time.
